Implemented DQ families (current framework):

1. `STATISTICAL_BASIC`
- `mean_std_shift_4sigma`, `rolling_zscore`, `zscore_mean_std`

2. `STATISTICAL_ROBUST`
- `robust_zscore`, `robust_zscore_mean_std`, `quantile_band`, `iqr`, `jump_spike`, `cusum_drift`, `rolling_ks`

3. `RULE_BASED`
- `stale_values`, `missing_gaps`, `rule_abs_shift_gt`

4. `ML_UNSUPERVISED` (feature-based, mostly PyOD/sklearn adapters)
- `pyod_iforest`, `pyod_lof`, `pyod_ecod`, `pyod_hbos`, `pyod_knn`, `pyod_copod`, `pyod_pca`, etc.

5. `ML_FORECAST_BASED` (time-series native)
- `arima_residual`, plus `adtk_*` and `merlion_*` adapters (with safe fallbacks)

6. `PEER_CROSS_GROUP`
- `peer_median_dev`, `peer_mad`, `peer_robust_z`, `peer_percentile`

7. `ENSEMBLE`
- `ensemble` with `max_score`, `weighted_avg`, `rank_aggregation`, `majority_vote`

How it flows:
- Each `check_id` outputs raw anomaly score + flag/severity.
- Scores are normalized across checks.
- Normalized scores become ML features for false-alarm / bad-outlier probability modeling.
